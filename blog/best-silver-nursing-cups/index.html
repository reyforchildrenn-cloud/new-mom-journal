Silverette, MoogCo, Willow, Momcozy, Koala Babycare, Go Mommy, and our own RFC cups all sell some version of the same idea, a small dome of silver worn over the nipple between feeds. The differences are real, but they are smaller and more specific than the marketing usually lets on.

What the silver purity numbers actually mean

Nearly every brand advertises a purity grade, and this is the single most confusing part of shopping for these. Here is what the numbers mean in plain terms:

  • 925 sterling silver is 92.5% silver, alloyed with other metals (usually copper) for added strength. This is the most common grade in jewelry and in several nursing cup brands, including Silverette and MoogCo's tri-laminate construction.
  • 950 silver is 95% silver, a step up in purity from sterling, used by brands like Willow.
  • 999 fine silver is 99.9% silver, about as pure as commercially available silver gets. It is softer and more malleable than sterling, which is why brands using it, including Momcozy and RFC, tend to favor rounded, hand-finished shapes over sharp-edged designs.
  • Tri-laminate (used by MoogCo and Koala Babycare) refers to a layered construction rather than a single purity grade, aiming to balance durability with a silver-rich surface.

What actually matters when you are choosing

Once you look past the purity numbers, a handful of practical questions matter more day to day:

  • Do you need multiple sizes? Breast and nipple size vary, and a cup that is too large can rub rather than protect. Brands offering more than one size (Momcozy, RFC) let you fit each side independently.
  • What is included? A carrying case matters if you are pumping at work or traveling. A cleaning cloth and a fit ring (to help the cup sit securely under a bra) are small details that save you buying separate accessories. RFC also includes a free breastfeeding e-book with every order, written to answer the practical questions that come up in the first weeks, which none of the other brands here currently offer.
  • Do the edges sit comfortably against your skin? Hand-finished, rounded edges make a real difference for comfort during long wear, regardless of purity grade.
  • Do you want a cream-free routine? All silver cups are designed to be worn without lanolin or other creams. If you currently rely on a cream, switching to any of these brands is a routine change worth planning for.

When silver nursing cups are not the right fit

In the interest of the same honesty this guide opened with: silver cups are not the right tool for every situation. If pain traces back to a latch problem, tongue-tie, or an active infection, a cup worn between feeds can make the gap between sessions more comfortable, but it will not fix the underlying cause, that still needs a lactation consultant or a doctor. Mothers with a known nickel allergy should double check purity and alloy content carefully, since not every brand on this list is nickel-free even at high silver purity. And if you already have a nipple care routine that is genuinely working well for you, lanolin, a hydrogel pad, simple air-drying, there is no requirement to switch, silver cups are one solid option among several, not the only correct answer.

Frequently asked questions

Is 999 silver better than 925 silver for nursing cups?

Neither grade is proven to heal faster than the other. 999 fine silver is softer and more malleable, which is why it is often hand-finished into rounded shapes. 925 sterling silver is harder and more scratch-resistant because of its added alloy. The choice mostly comes down to feel and how the cup is shaped, not a documented difference in results.

Do silver nursing cups actually work?

Many mothers report meaningful relief from wearing them between feeds, and silver's natural antimicrobial properties have a long history in wound care generally. They are not a substitute for fixing an underlying latch problem, which is the most common actual cause of nipple pain, so they work best alongside a good latch rather than instead of one.

How do I clean silver nursing cups?

Rinse with warm water daily and dry thoroughly, since trapped moisture is what causes tarnishing or staining, not the silver itself. A soft paste of baking soda and water can restore shine if a cup discolors over time. Avoid harsh chemical cleaners, which can be more abrasive than the silver needs.

Can I wear silver nursing cups while sleeping?

Most brands, including RFC, design these for wear between feeds, which for many mothers includes overnight. A well-fitted cup that sits securely under a soft, supportive bra should not shift much during sleep, but if a cup feels loose or is sliding, it is a sign that the fit or size needs adjusting.

Do I need a different size for each breast?

It is common for one breast to be slightly larger than the other, especially in the first weeks of breastfeeding. Brands that offer more than one size, including Momcozy and RFC, let you match each side independently rather than compromising with a single size for both.

How long do silver nursing cups last?

Silver does not wear out the way a disposable pad does, so most brands market these as a one-time purchase that lasts through breastfeeding and weaning, and often for a second child if stored properly in a case between uses.
